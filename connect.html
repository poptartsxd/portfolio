Get Connected

UX/UI Design, Research, Prototyping | Aug â€™16 - Present | Partner Project

Get Connected is a standalone TV application that aides students in meeting their peers and forming groups. It is placed in a collaborative space and fosters natural and organic communication.

My Role: UX/UI Designer & Researcher
Methods: Persona, Storyboard, Wireframe, Survey, Interview, Focus Group, Affinity Mapping, Interactive Prototyping
Tools: Sketch, Framer

OVERVIEW

Our goal was to help new students meet their peers in a natural setting. Through our research, we found that students had difficulty getting to know their classmates because there was little chance for them to meet organically. Get Connected helps foster dialogue through thought-provoking and class-related questions, as well as daily trivia games to allow students to meet new people. Because it is placed in a centralized physical location, it creates a collaborative environment.

I focused on conducting user research, designing the user flow, creating the UI of the application, and making the prototype interactive with Framer.

PROCESS


RESEARCH

Target User Group

Our target users are new HCI students. Because HCI has a lot of project-based classes, we thought that it was a good area to start before expanding to other programs. Having a majority of project-based classes increases the pressure to meet new people to form groups.

Survey

We conducted an initial survey with two questions:

  1. At what part of collaboration did you have the most problems with as an individual?
  2. At what part of collaboration did you have the most problems with as a group?

Our results indicated that over 50% of respondents ranked finding peers that were compatible as the most difficult. In addition, almost 50% of respondents had difficulty agreeing on similar ideas within their project groups.

Interviews
  • Method: semi-structured interviews
  • Who: 10 first-year students
  • Goal: to better understand how students were adjusting to the new environment and meeting new peers
Focus Group
  • Method: moderator focus group
  • Who: 4 first-year students in same group
  • Goal: to better understand how each member of the group met and decided to work together
Problem Space

We took our research data and mapped out the main points on an affinity diagram. We found two main user needs:

  1. Students need more opportunities to get to know their fellow classmates
  2. Students need more time to see who they have better chemistry with in order to form a successful group

Most of our interviewees mentioned "personality" as being the first thing they look for when forming a group. In order to understand someone's personality, they need the chance to get to know them better.

DESIGN

Idea Generation

Based on the user needs, our solution should:

  • Encompass both technology and physical context
  • Foster organic conversation between students within the program

After brainstorming and coming up with multiple ideas, we decided on the concept of a physical device placed in a centralized location to encourage dialogue between students.

The application should have the following features:

  • Be large enough as multiple people will be looking at one screen
  • Have some sort of collaborative activity that will get people to work together
  • Be able to spur conversation without making users feel like it is too forced

Initial User Testing

We conducted user testing with our paper prototypes and discovered the following findings:

  • Users felt that "News" section did not relate to school information
  • Users felt that there should be a timer to let players know when time is up for each question
Design Decision #1: Standby screen sticky notes

The standby screen is designed to start dialogue between students in the collaborative space. When they see a question, they can tap to add their response. In addition, as they are adding, another student could come by and start talking to them about the question.

Allowing for these "sticky notes" to be added allows for dialogue to be started even when there is no one else around to talk to. People coming by will be able to read the question and responses.

Design Decision #2: Home screen dashboard

The home screen is a dashboard that has four main features:

  • Question of the day: to keep the question from the standby screen visible
  • Upcoming events: to display program-related events
  • Students looking for groups: list of students seeking to form groups
  • Trivia: game to allow students in the collaborative space to meet new people and work together to solve questions

Having a dashboard allows quick access to relevant information.

Design Decision #3: List of students looking for groups

This section displays a list of students who are looking to form a group. Skills and experiences are displayed to facilitate the formation process. New students may not know how to reach out to their peers yet so this serves as a place for them to start.

This list is similar to a "help wanted" ad, allowing new students to easily learn more about their peers and giving them the chance to reach out to them.

Design Decision #4: Daily trivia feature

There will be a daily trivia game that will prompt nearby students in the space to find other students also working in the area to play trivia. This increases the communication between students because it creates the opportunity for people to meet.

Because this device is placed in a centralized location, meeting opportunities do not feel as forced and students can easily find those around to play the game with.

NEXT STEPS

User Testing

We want to test our system with real students by placing a large screen in the student lounge. Challenges will include acquiring a large screen that can be placed vertically (i.e. TV screen) and displaying dynamic data as we currently only have a prototype.

Reflection
  • Designing for a big screen is different than designing for mobile/desktop. This application would be placed on a giant screen so visibility from afar was an important consideration as we were designing the interface.
  • It's easy to get lost in adding more and more features. Originally, we had a bunch of features and ideas we wanted to add to the application (i.e. a News section). However, once we focused on the user needs and the design's purpose, we were able to narrow it down to the core functionalities.
